Why Cusco?

Cusco, City of the Inca Empire

Reasons to Visit Cusco, The Capital of the Peruvian AndesCusco, Peru, is famous as the ancient capital of the Inca Empire, a gateway to Machu Picchu, and a stunning blend of Incan and Spanish colonial history, known for its unique stone architecture, vibrant markets (like San Pedro), rich Andean culture, and as a base for treks like the Inca Trail. It's a UNESCO World Heritage site celebrated for its historical sites (Sacsayhuaman, Qorikancha), beautiful churches (Cusco Cathedral), artisan neighborhoods (San Blas), Andean textiles, and delicious traditional cuisine.

The city features a unique architectural blend, with Spanish colonial buildings, baroque churches, and mansions built directly on top of impressive, earthquake-resistant Inca stone foundations and walls. The Qorikancha (Temple of the Sun), with the Santo Domingo Convent built over it, is a prime example of this historical layering.
